## vector db use case

A traditional keyword-based search system would be insufficient for querying large legal documents such as 500-page contracts. Keyword search relies on exact term matching and fails to capture semantic meaning, making it ineffective when different legal terminology or phrasing is used. For example, a clause related to “termination” might not explicitly contain the keyword but could still be relevant, leading to missed results.
A vector database addresses this limitation by enabling semantic search through embeddings. Each document, paragraph, or clause is converted into a high-dimensional vector representation using transformer-based models. When a lawyer submits a query in natural language, it is also embedded into a vector, and similarity search (e.g., cosine similarity) is performed to retrieve the most contextually relevant sections, regardless of exact wording.
This approach significantly improves recall and precision, especially in complex domains like legal text where language is nuanced and varied. Additionally, vector databases support efficient nearest-neighbor search, allowing scalable retrieval even across large document corpora.
In a production system, a hybrid approach is often optimal—combining keyword filtering for structured constraints with vector search for semantic understanding. This ensures both accuracy and performance. Overall, vector databases play a critical role in enabling intelligent, context-aware search systems that enhance productivity and decision-making in legal workflows.
